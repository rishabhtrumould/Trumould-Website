When to Choose 3D Printing

  • Concept validation: Test form, fit, and basic function before investing in tooling.
  • Design iteration: Print multiple versions in parallel to compare and optimize designs rapidly.
  • Functional testing: SLS nylon and SLA tough resin parts withstand real-world mechanical testing.
  • Low-volume production: 1-500 parts without mold investment. Ideal for pilot runs.
  • Complex geometries: Internal channels, lattice structures, and undercuts that are impossible to mold.
  • Bridge production: Keep your launch on schedule while injection mold tooling is built.
